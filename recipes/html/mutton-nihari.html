Mutton Nihari

Description

Nihari is a slow-cooked mutton stew that originated in the Mughal kitchens of the Indian subcontinent. Deep, beefy spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and nutmeg melt into the tender meat over hours of cooking. Nihari is rich, oily, and traditionally eaten at breakfast with naan, though many now enjoy it for lunch or dinner. The dish is indulgent, powerful, and known for its medicinal warmth during winters.

Ingredients

  • Mutton (shank or bones)
  • Onions
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Nihari masala
  • Flour (for thickening)
  • Salt
  • Water or beef stock
  • Oil or ghee
  • Lemon, ginger, green chilies (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat oil, add onions and sauté until golden.
  2. Add mutton and fry with ginger garlic paste.
  3. Add nihari masala and cook for 10–15 minutes.
  4. Add water or stock and simmer on low for 4–6 hours.
  5. Mix flour with water, add to stew to thicken.
  6. Garnish with sliced ginger, green chilies, and lemon.
  7. Serve hot with naan.
